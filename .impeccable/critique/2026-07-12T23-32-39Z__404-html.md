## Overall Impression

Visually this is the most disciplined page on the site — zero new anti-patterns, full reuse of proven brand components. But it's also the page most likely to end a session with zero brand affinity: no social links, no alternate routes, and the one place where the writing sounds like anyone else's error page.

## What's Working

1. Disciplined reuse of the design system: logo lockup, eyebrow kicker, gold pill CTA, and the navy scrim-over-photo technique are all lifted verbatim from patterns already proven elsewhere.
2. Real local photography (Punta Lara aerial/coastal shot), not stock or a placeholder panel.
3. Accessibility inherited for free: the global focus-visible gold ring applies automatically to both links on this page.

## Priority Issues

- **[P2] Dead end relative to the site's own stated success metric.** PRODUCT.md defines success as visitors following Turismo Ensenada's social accounts (linked in the footer elsewhere) — this page has no footer, no nav, no social links at all. A visitor who bounces here has zero chance of converting on the site's own primary goal. → `/impeccable clarify` or `/impeccable polish`
- **[P2] Copy carries no brand voice.** The one sentence of body copy reads as generic/institutional — exactly what the brand's anti-references warn against — while every other section's eyebrow sits above copy with real personality. → `/impeccable clarify`
- **[P3] Single recovery path only.** No links to likely intended destinations (En Fotos, Cultura, Historia, En Vivo) or a search affordance, only "Volver al inicio." → `/impeccable clarify`
- **[P3] Unverified overlap risk on short viewports.** `.not-found .brand` is `position: absolute; top: 32px` with no mobile-specific override found; on a short landscape-phone viewport the centered content could push up under the logo. Flagged as "verify in a real browser," not a certain bug. → `/impeccable adapt`
- **[P3] Two near-identical golds (pre-existing, not introduced here).** CTA uses `--brand-yellow` (#ffd43d) while DESIGN.md's "Regla del Resaltador" names `#ffcf4a`/`#ffcf2f` as the highlighter accent — consistent with how `--brand-yellow` is already used elsewhere, just surfaces the underlying token duplication here too.

This page may also be Jordan's very first impression of the brand's voice if they arrive via a stale external link before ever seeing the homepage, and the copy here is the flattest, least "río, cultura, historia, orgullo local" text on the domain.

## Questions to Consider

- If the project's own success metric is "visitor keeps following our social accounts," why is the page most likely to end a session with zero brand affinity the one page with zero links to those accounts?
- Three other sections on this domain prove the brand can write copy with personality under an eyebrow — why does the error page default to the same neutral phrasing every template 404 uses?
